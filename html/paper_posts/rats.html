irRadiATed rats Around Rotating RAdio Transients Revolving About The RunAway T-Tauri Star

Astronomers have pondered the nature of Rotating RAdio Transients (RRAT). They have scrutunised the RunAway T-Tauri Stars (RATTS). But never before have they considered RRATs revolving about RATTSs (RRATRATTS). In this interdisciplinary work, we investigate formation mechanisms for RRATRATTSs and the prospect of detecting irradiated rats on planets around them.

Rats. For many, that word brings to mind a scurrilous, unkempt little rodent, prowling the streets of their favourite city. But not astronomers. No, they looked beyond this simple view and thought to ask the question: what more could a rat be?

Why of course, they stated, a rat could be a runaway T-Tauri star (RATTS, e.g. Sterzik+1995), a young protostar, heartlessly ejected from its parent molecular cloud, set free into the depths of space! Later, others cried that a rat could be a rotating radio transient (RRAT, e.g. McLaughlin+2006), a rapidly spinning neutron star (NS) living as a remnant of its former self, emitting momentary radio bursts.

And now, at last, with this work we contemplate how these rats may come together as a Rotating Radio Transient Revolving About The RunAway T-Tauri Star (RRATRATTS).

3-body encounter animation

The video below shows an animation of the 3-body encounter plot from the paper. I ran this simulation using REBOUND to produce an example scenario of how a binary RRATRATTS could arise.

We initialise a binary containing a $1.4 \, \rm{M_\odot}$ neutron star and $0.3 \, \rm{M_\odot}$ main sequence star with an orbital separation of $2.5 \, \rm{AU}$, moving with a centre of mass velocity of approximately $30 \, \rm{km} \, {s^{-1}}$ (motivated by the assumption that the NS underwent an electron-capture supernova). We then add a RATTS of mass $0.9 \, \rm{M_\odot}$, moving with a velocity of approximately $45 \, \rm{km} \, {s^{-1}}$ (approximately following the characteristics of UJT-1 discovered by Marti+2023) towards the binary and trace the evolution of the system.
